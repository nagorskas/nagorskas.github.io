(Not) To Pass By (2020–2023) is an analog landscape photography series. The series portrays landscapes that are empty and mundane, something that is rarely seen in the genre. These landscapes can be seen in the countryside of Lithuania and are encountered near transit roads. As such, they are most often just passed by, either on foot or by car, and rarely pull the attention of the passerby. The series aims to invite the viewer to take a look at these seldom looked-at views and find something interesting about them, something to help the viewer create their own story, their own aesthetic experience. This experience is divided into three parts, each of them distinct in style and function.
(Not) To Pass By III (2022–2023), also known as To Stay, unlike the previous two parts, is a piece of video art. While the shots are still static, reminiscent of the photographs seen prior, the background and the landscapes portrayed now have subtle movement that requires the viewer to take a longer and more concentrated look at the image. Mirroring the viewer is a figure standing in the middle of the landscape, looking away from the camera and towards the landscape, acting as a visual anchor and representing the viewer in the image itself.
